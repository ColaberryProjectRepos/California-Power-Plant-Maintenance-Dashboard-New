 1. Accuracy (95%) An accuracy of 95% means that only 5% of the predictions were incorrect when tested on the validation dataset. This includes both false positives  and false negatives .
2.F1 Score (95%) An F1 score of 95% suggests that the model has an excellent balance between precision and recall, correctly identifying most positive cases while maintaining low false positives and false negatives.
3.Precision (96.8%) A precision of 96.8% means that when the model predicts a positive outcome, it is correct 96.8% of the time, with only 3.2% of positive predictions being false positives.
3.Recall (93.3%) A recall of 93.3% indicates that the model successfully identifies 93.3% of all actual positive cases, but misses 6.7% of true positives (false negatives).
